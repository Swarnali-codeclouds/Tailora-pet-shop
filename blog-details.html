5 Signs Your Dog is Trying to Tell You Something (and What to Do About It)

By

  • Taillora Pet Experts
  • June 5, 2025
  • 4 min read

Pets don’t speak our language — but they’re always talking. From tail wags to ear flicks, your dog’s behavior tells a story. In this post, we decode common signals that often go unnoticed and what they might mean for your pup’s well-being.

  • 1. Excessive Licking

    A wagging tail is a classic sign of happiness, but did you know the direction and speed can reveal even more? A slow wag to the right often indicates contentment, while a fast wag to the left may signal excitement or anxiety.This can signal anxiety, boredom, or even a skin issue. Find out when to worry and what calming activities help.

  • 2. Tail Tucks or Trembling

    If your dog suddenly cowers or shakes, it could be fear or discomfort — we explain how to comfort them or spot signs of illness.

  • 3. Pacing or Circling

    Is your dog restless or walking in patterns? It might be mental stimulation they need — or a sign to visit the vet.

  • 4. Avoiding Eye Contact

    Dogs use eye contact to bond — or avoid conflict. Here's how to know the difference.

  • 5. Sleeping More Than Usual

    Changes in sleep patterns can signal age, stress, or hidden health issues. Here’s when to monitor and when to act.

“Dogs love routine. Sudden behavior shifts often point to changes in their environment or health.”

– Dr. Meera Banerjee, Taillora Veterinary Advisor
